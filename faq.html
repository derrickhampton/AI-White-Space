HiddenChars FAQ: Zero-Width Space Detector & AI Humanizer

HiddenChars is a free online zero-width space detector and AI text humanizer. Below you’ll find answers explaining how the hidden character removal tool works and how the Humanize feature rewrites robotic AI-generated text into natural, readable language.

What is a zero-width space?

A zero-width space (U+200B) is an invisible Unicode character used for formatting. It does not display visually but can break search indexing, formatting, copying, and SEO performance. Many AI systems and copy-paste workflows introduce hidden Unicode characters like zero-width joiners, non-breaking spaces (NBSP), and word joiners.

How does the Hidden Character Detector work?

The HiddenChars tool scans your pasted text in your browser and detects invisible Unicode whitespace such as:

  • Zero-width space (U+200B)
  • Zero-width joiner (U+200D)
  • Zero-width non-joiner (U+200C)
  • Non-breaking space (U+00A0)
  • Word joiner (U+2060)

When detected, the tool highlights hidden characters and allows you to remove invisible Unicode safely before downloading clean text.

Why do invisible characters matter for SEO?

Hidden whitespace can disrupt search engine indexing, keyword matching, copy-paste accuracy, and web form submissions. Removing invisible Unicode characters ensures clean HTML, accurate content indexing, and improved technical SEO performance.

How does the AI Humanizer tool work?

The Humanize tool rewrites stiff or robotic AI-generated content using case-preserving transformations and context-aware rule-based rewriting. It improves readability by:

  • Replacing robotic transition phrases
  • Reducing repetitive sentence structures
  • Applying natural contractions
  • Softening long run-on sentences

The result is clearer, more natural-sounding writing while preserving meaning.

Does HiddenChars store my text?

No. Text processing occurs in your browser. HiddenChars does not intentionally store or log pasted text from the detection or humanize tools.
